mainContent (h3 sections):

### Project Overview
Felgilab restores and repairs car rims in Warsaw. I'd previously worked with them on SEO and conversion improvements to their existing MODX site — this project was a full rebuild: new architecture, new design, and a new technical foundation on WordPress, built to remove the performance and usability ceiling the old platform had reached.

### Why a Full Rebuild, Not Another Round of Fixes
The MODX site had a hard ceiling: poor performance, no meaningful conversion path for visitors, and an admin experience that made the client dependent on a developer for basic content changes. Patching around those constraints indefinitely wasn't the right call — the business needed a new technical foundation it could actually grow on.

### Designing Around Real User Behavior
Before touching design or code, I researched how people actually search for rim restoration services and how they behave once they land on a site — what they look for, what makes them leave, what makes them trust a business enough to submit an inquiry. The site's structure, from the homepage layout to the service page architecture, was built around that research rather than assumptions.

### The Feature That Changed Everything: Photo-Based Quotes
The centerpiece of the new lead form is simple but effective: customers upload photos of their damaged or scratched rims directly in the inquiry form and receive an estimated price and timeline. It removes the friction of a phone call just to get a rough idea of cost — and it worked. The large majority of visitors now use it, and it became the biggest single driver of lead volume on the new site.

### Order Status Lookup & Real-Time Store Status
Two smaller but meaningful additions: customers can check on their order's status by entering their phone number, without needing to call and interrupt the shop floor — and a real-time indicator shows whether the business is currently open, pulled from actual business hours rather than a static label.

### Reaching a Multilingual Customer Base
Warsaw has a large Ukrainian- and Russian-speaking population alongside international residents, and Felgilab's customer base reflects that. The site is fully built in four languages — Polish, English, Russian, and Ukrainian — so visitors can research services, understand pricing, and submit a quote request in their own language rather than hitting a translation barrier at the moment they're deciding whether to trust the business.

### Built as a Custom WordPress Theme — No Page Builder
The site runs on a custom-built WordPress theme with a lean, modern build process (Vite, minimal JS/CSS, no jQuery) — not a page-builder plugin stack. That keeps the site fast and maintainable, and it's paired with a CMS structure that lets the client edit any existing page or create new ones without touching code.

### Performance, SEO & Technical Foundation
The rebuild focused as much on technical health as on visuals: mobile PageSpeed scores now sit at 92 (Performance), 91 (Accessibility), 96 (Best Practices), and 100 (SEO) — up from roughly 50 on the old site. That included structured data work (including LocalBusiness markup for local search and Maps visibility) and modern image format optimization, alongside a dedicated architecture of service-specific landing pages for search visibility across the range of services Felgilab offers.

### Key Takeaways
A redesign only pays off when it's built around how real customers actually behave — the photo-upload quote form is the clearest example here, a single well-placed feature that measurably changed lead volume. Pairing that with a genuinely fast, technically sound foundation and a CMS the client can actually use turned the site from a liability into a working sales channel.
